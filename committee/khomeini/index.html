Death of Khomeini


Background Guide Coming in Jan. 2020


Greetings delegates, and welcome to the Committee on the Death of Imam Khomeini! This committee will feature many things, among them, dealing with the aftermath of the Iran-Iraq War, choosing a successor to Imam Khomeini, and increasing the hegemonic influence of Iran as a whole in an increasingly Western world. As you all know, Imam Khomeini was (and continues to be) the most important person in the history of the Islamic Republic of Iran. Khomeini was the most prominent leader of the Islamic Revolution, and he and his followers successfully overthrew the Shah in 1979 and brought with them the birth of a new theocratic nation. Unfortunately, Khomeini’s fire for the Revolution burned out in 1989, and the leader passed away on June 3rd after suffering through five heart attacks in ten days and overall ill health for many years. The people of Iran were so stricken with grief at the death of their dear leader that the leader’s corpse was almost lost in a funeral procession after civilians frantically reached for it to connect with their hero one last time. Over 10 million people attended the funeral. In order for the Islamic Republic to live on, Khomeini needs to designate an heir. The process of finding a successor must be done with great care and adherence to the requirements set by Islam, or velayat-e faqih. Two contenders currently exist for this spot: Hussein-Ali Montazeri and Ali Khamenei, however, the choice for succession will ultimately be based on the perceived wishes of Khomeini and the approval by the Assembly of Experts. In this committee particularly, the debate is over who rightfully deserves the position, as both candidates have previously been mired in select controversies. Additionally, the committee will have to handle relations with encircling Gulf nations who see strategic opportunity in allying with the United States, whose cultures and customs are antithetical to the religion of Islam. Strike your balances carefully, as the fate of the Republic exists in your hands.
