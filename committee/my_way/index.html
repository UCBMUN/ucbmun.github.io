Strang(l)ers in the Night: The Mystery of the My Way Killings

Background Guide coming soon!


In the midst of Manila's bustling, vibrant streets, a haunting melody of death strikes up a chilling note. A series of ruthless murders have occurred all over the city, and a peculiar pattern begins to emerge: Frank Sinatra's 'My Way' playing eerily in the background at each of the crime scenes. Yet, in the city's cacophony, it's uncertain whether these deaths share a dark connection, or if the song is just an eerie coincidence. As dedicated police investigators, delegates will traverse the treacherous labyrinth that is Manila, facing a series of baffling murders that echo through the streets of the Philippines. Navigating through a maze of danger and deception, delegates must put their skills to the test, deciphering cryptic clues, examining enigmatic suspects, and evading becoming another verse in this bloody ballad. Can you unravel the mystery before the song fades into silence? Will you hit all the right notes? Or will your voice waver and crack when the time comes?




Milad Brown

Chair

Hi Everyone!

I am honored to welcome each and every one of you to UCBMUN’s My Way Killings committee! Working to prepare this committee with Anousha and Maanas has been a great experience. Committees like this one, that let us interact with the mysterious and hypothetical, are amazing experiences because they allow us to truly think outside of the box and push our creativity and deduction to theor limits. Even outside the subject matter of our specific committee, I hope you all enjoy being surrounded by peers of similar energy and passions. In my time at Berkeley, I’ve seen time and again that MUN has a unique value and I hope you can make the most of it.

In our committee you will be confronted with many scenarios that are uncertain. It is up to you to piece together the clues you are given and form a coherent and compelling strategy for the committee. Be ready to bring out of the box thinking and plenty of good vibes—the standard MUN playbook will only take you so far in this committee. If you can help make committee more fun, happy, or interesting, that is a win in my book!

As I end this letter, I want to offer a last round of appreciations and acknowledgements. To my fellow committee staffers, I send my deepest gratitude for working with me through this process. To our Secretariat, especially our amazing SG Harsh, DG Sehr, and USG Maanas, my most sincere thanks for your dedication as well. Also, to see if anybody reads this far in the chair letter, I’ll Venmo $5 to the first person who says “ponyboy” in committee. As to you, the delegates, I eagerly await seeing you in action and wish you the best of luck!

Sincerely, Milad Brown Chair, My Way Killings
