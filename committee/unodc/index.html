UN Office on Drugs and Crime

Topic A: The Fight Against Doping in Sports

Topic B: Combatting Trafficking in South and Southeast Asia

This is a dual–delegation committee.

Background Guide coming soon.

unodc@ucbmun.org


Welcome to the UN Office on Drugs and Crime, or UNODC. This committee takes place in the present day, and will address either the topic of doping or human trafficking in South and Southeast Asia. The issue of doping has been an important issue in the past several years, with cases such as banning US athletes from Olympic competition due to recreational drugs to large scandals such as state-sponsored Russian doping showing the importance of addressing anti-doping policy. Human trafficking is an extremely important topic that falls under the purview of the UNODC, and trafficking, including sex trafficking and child trafficking, is especially prevalent in the Indo–Pacific due to high levels of poverty and corruption. As members of UNODC, delegat–s are expected to use creative thinking and collaborative planning to create meaningful policy that creates short–term relief and long–term solutions, even when strategies call for systemic changes. Sports act as a soft power in international relations, and trafficking is a modern epidemic that disrupts and dehumanizes the lives of its victims, so we hope that in our time together, we are able to holistically improve humankind.
