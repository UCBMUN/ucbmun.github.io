Background Guide


The Group of Latin America and Caribbean Countries, or GRULAC, is one of five geographical regional groups within the United Nations. Although GRULAC does serve as a regional forum, it has taken a much more significant role in the sense that its member countries can coordinate together to back certain policies or regulations in the UN General Assembly. GRULAC consists of about 17% of UN membership and thus has substantial power when it comes to bloc voting in the organ bodies. The topics that delegates will be debating about throughout this committee include the refugee crisis, narco-terrorism, organ trafficking, power grid infrastructure and natural disasters in the region.
