COVID-19 Policies

At UCBMUN XXVI, we are committed to protecting the health and safety of all delegates and all staffers. We plan to follow the CDC, state of California, City of San Francisco, University of California, Berkeley, and the Hilton Financial District’s guidelines in order to best prevent the spread of COVID-19. If you have any questions or concerns, please reach out to secretarygeneral@ucbmun.org.

  1. All delegates will be required to show proof of vaccination (delegates must be fully vaccinated which means at least two weeks past the second dose of a two-shot vaccine or two weeks past the first dose of a one-shot vaccine). Additionally, UCBMUN XXVI highly encourages those eligible to obtain a booster shot before arriving at conference. Proof of vaccination must be uploaded prior to arrival and can be uploaded at this form.
  2. All delegates will be required to show proof of a negative COVID-19 test taken within 72 hours of the start of conference. We have a strong preference for PCR testing but will accept antigen tests as well. Tests must be taken 2/28-3/3 but we encourage delegates to budget at least 1 or 2 days to receive results. Test results will be presented at registration by the head delegate or faculty advisor. Delegations will be banned from entering committee session I unless every member of a delegation has presented a negative test result.
    1. An exception to this rule is any delegate who has tested positive and recovered from COVID-19 in the past 90 days. Delegates who have had COVID in the past 90 days may submit proof of positive test and/or a signed letter from a healthcare provider, public health official, or university-issued message documenting their recovery. However, delegates must be past the 10 days from an initial positive test to attend UCBMUN.
  3. All delegates will be required to wear masks in public indoor spaces, including committee and other UCBMUN sponsored events, regardless of vaccination status. Masks must be worn over the mouth and nose. Surgical, KN95, and N95 masks are strongly encouraged as opposed to cloth masks. Neck gaiters, scarves, and bandanas will not be allowed. Anyone refusing to comply with the mask mandate will be asked to leave the conference and could jeopardize their team’s consideration for delegation awards.
  4. Mandatory Symptom Screener. Prior to the beginning of every committee session, delegates will be asked to fill out a symptom screener. Until a delegate has filled this out, they will not be eligible to participate in committee.
  5. Delegates Experiencing Symptoms of COVID-19
    1. Anyone displaying symptoms of COVID-19 will be asked to isolate and leave the conference, regardless of vaccination status.
    2. Symptoms of COVID-19 include but are not limited to fever, loss of smell or taste, shortness of breath, sore or scratchy throat, headache, persistent cough, congestion, runny or stuffy nose, fatigue, and nausea.
    3. Delegates who are experiencing symptoms are required to purchase a rapid COVID-19 test. If the test is negative, delegates can return to committee session provided their symptoms do not get worse and that they do not have a fever. Delegates will also be asked to notify the rest of their delegation and their chairs of these symptoms.
  6. Protocol for Delegates Who Test Positive While At Conference
    1. Delegates who test positive for COVID must email secretarygeneral@ucbmun.org with their name, email, school, committee allocation, hotel room number (if applicable), head delegate or faculty advisor contact information, and roommate contact information.
    2. After a positive test result, a delegate must isolate in their room and will be barred from attending UCBMUN events.
    3. Roommates of a delegate who tests positive must also purchase rapid tests. If they too test positive, they will also be asked to isolate. If they are negative and asymptomatic, they can return to committee session but must secure separate accommodations and will be asked to continually monitor symptoms.
    4. Failure for a delegate who tests positive to follow the appropriate protocol will result in that delegation being disqualified from any and all awards.
  7. Delegates requesting refunds due to COVID-19 infection will be refunded in full.
  8. COVID-19 policies from the Hilton hotel can be found at https://www.hilton.com/en/corporate/cleanstay/.
  9. Delegates may be subject to temperature checks. If delegates have a fever, they will be asked to isolate and not attend committee sessions.
  10. Failure to comply with any and all UCBMUN COVID-19 restrictions will result in removal from the conference and disqualification from awards.
